Hi!

I'm Umutesa Munyurangabo, a junior electrical and computer engineer driven by a passion for merging engineering with empathy to create meaningful solutions within the African context. My academic interests include computer vision, deep learning and natural language processing.

About Me

As a woman, I advocate for approaches that reflect the broader social contexts women represent— this means challenging biases and grounding decisions in empathy and ethics. Engineering at its best is not only about creating effective solutions—it involves understanding the human stories behind the challenges to ensure solutions align with people's experiences, values and identities.

At the moment, I'm pursuing my Master of Science degree at the University of Cape Town (UCT), where my thesis, Vision-Only Socially Compliant Navigation Robotics, explores how computer vision and machine learning can advance the way robots navigate socially complex environments. I hold a Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Cape Town. This site serves as a living archive of my professional journey—and a glimpse into my creative pursuits along the way.

My areas of interest include:

  • Machine Learning: Particularly deep learning.
  • Autonomous Navigation in Robotics and Vehicles: Safe and Compliant navigation which lies at the heart of my master's research (something I've spent countless hours refining).
  • Computer Vision: My interest extends to the contextual understanding within visual data.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): I'm drawn to the beautiful messiness of human language—the ambiguity, bias, cultural backgrounds, and their misinterpretations. I am interested in a neural approach to NLP, integrating visual, auditory and textual cues to improve the understanding of human communication.
  • Audio Signal Processing: With a background in Electrical Engineering I am interest in signal processing for speech and audio recognition./li>
  • Empathy and Psychology in Engineering: Understanding the human stories behind the data, models and decisions we make is what drives meaningful innovation.
  • Women in STEM: Representing, and Supporting women in STEM fields.

Projects

Here's a showcase of my ongoing work:

Time Series Prediction for USD/ZAR Exchange using Deep Learning Models

This project delves into deep learning models to analyze and forecast USD/ZAR movements using historical data from Yahoo Finance. By comparing Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs), and Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs), I aim to uncover patterns and generate accurate predictions that can assist in making informed decisions about the fanacial market.

Image to Text Synthesizer

This project investigates different deep learning models for converting images into text, mainly Diffusion models, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The goal is to assess accuracy, efficiency, and applicability across various real-world scenarios, using CIFAR-10, a widely recognized dataset for image classification.

Optimizing GPT-2 Chatbpt using RAG, Fine-Tuning and Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback for Answering Questions about food Receipes

This project focuses on fine-tuning GPT-2 Medium to create a chatbot dedicated to answering questions about food recipes.
