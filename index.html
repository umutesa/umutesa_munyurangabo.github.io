Hi, there!

I'm Umutesa Munyurangabo, a Junior Electrical and Computer Engineer driven by a passion for merging engineering with empathy to create meaningful solutions within the African context. My academic interests include computer vision, deep learning and natural language processing.

About Me

As a woman, I advocate for approaches that reflect the broader social contexts women represent— this means challenging biases and grounding decisions in empathy and ethics. Engineering at its best is not only about creating effective solutions—it involves understanding the human stories behind the challenges to ensure solutions align with people's experiences, values and identities.

At the moment, I'm pursuing my Master of Science degree at the University of Cape Town (UCT), where my thesis, Vision-Only Socially Compliant Navigation Robotics, explores how computer vision and machine learning can advance the way robots navigate socially complex environments. I hold a Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Cape Town. This site serves as a living archive of my professional journey—and a glimpse into my creative pursuits along the way.

My areas of interest include:

  • Machine Learning: Particularly deep learning.
  • Autonomous Navigation in Robotics and Vehicles: Safe and Compliant navigation which lies at the heart of my master's research (something I've spent countless hours refining).

Time Series Prediction for USD/ZAR Exchange Rate using Deep Learning Models

This project delves into deep learning models to analyze and forecast USD/ZAR Exchange Rate using historical data from Yahoo Finance. By comparing Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs), and Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs), I aim to uncover patterns and generate accurate predictions that can assist in making informed decisions about the financial market.

Image to Text Synthesizer

This project investigates different deep learning models for converting images into text, specifically diffusion models, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The goal is to assess each model's performance using the CIFAR-10 dataset, a widely recognized dataset for image classification.

Optimizing LLM using RAG, Fine-Tuning and Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback for Answering Questions about South African Recipes

Chatbots have taken center stage in digital interaction, revolutionizing business, customer service, education, and daily assistance. But the generic models don’t always provide the specialized knowledge users need. This project focuses on fine-tuning a LLM model. i.e., GPT-2-medium to create a chatbot dedicated to answering questions about food recipes. By incorporating Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and user reinforcement feedback, I enhance its ability to provide better responses to questions about the food recipes.
