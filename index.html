Hi!

I’m Umutesa Munyurangabo, a junior electrical and computer engineer driven by a passion for merging engineering with empathy to create meaningful solutions with the Africa context. My academic interests include computer vision, deep learning and natural language processing.

About Me

As a woman, I advocate for approaches that reflect the broader social contexts women represent— this means challenging biases and grounding decisions in empathy and ethics. Engineering at its best is not only about creating effective solutions—it involves understanding the human stories behind the challenges and ensuring that solutions align with people's experiences, values and identities.

At the moment, I’m pursuing my Master of Science degree at the University of Cape Town (UCT), where my thesis, Vision-Only Socially Compliant Navigation Robotics, explores how computer vision and machine learning can advance the way robots navigate socially complex environments.

This site serves as a living archive of my professional journey—and a glimpse into my creative pursuits along the way.

If you're curious, my areas of interest include:

  • Machine Learning: Particularly deep learning.
  • Autonomous Navigation in Robotics and Vehicles: Safe and Compiliant navigation which lies at the heart of my master’s research (something I've spent countless hours refining).
  • Computer Vision: My interest extends to the contextual understanding within visual data.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): I’m drawn to the beautiful messiness of human language—the ambiguity, bias, cultural backgrounds, and their misinterpretations. I am interested in a neural approach to NLP, integrating visual, auditory and textual cues to improve the understanding of human communication.
  • Empathy and Psychology in Engineering: Understanding the human stories behind the data, models and decisions we make is what drives meaningful innovation.
  • Advocacy for Women in STEM: Representing, and supporting women in STEM fields.
  • Reading: I’m trying to rekindle my love for fiction. Thus far, The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho stands out as a favorite.
